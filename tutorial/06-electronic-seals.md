# 06. Electronic Seals

## Introduction

Electronic seals are often confused with electronic signatures, but they are not the same thing. Both belong to the eIDAS trust framework, yet they serve different purposes.

## What an Electronic Seal Is

An electronic seal is primarily linked to a legal person or entity rather than to a natural person.

Its main role is to support:

- origin
- integrity
- documentary authenticity at entity level

## Difference from an Electronic Signature

The signature is normally associated with a natural person and with that person's act of signing.

The seal is more oriented toward:

- organizations
- institutional issuance
- automated or large-scale documentary flows

## Why Seals Matter

Electronic seals are especially useful in contexts such as:

- issuing corporate documents
- automating documentary output
- certifying the origin of information from an organization

## Qualified Electronic Seals

As with other trust services, the qualified framework also exists for seals. A qualified electronic seal belongs to the reinforced legal structure of eIDAS.

## Practical View

One of the most common mistakes is to use the words signature and seal as if they were interchangeable. In legal and technical terms, they are not.

## Summary

An electronic seal is the entity-oriented counterpart within the eIDAS trust ecosystem. It is especially relevant for institutional origin and integrity.
