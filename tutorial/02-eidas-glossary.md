# 02. Basic eIDAS Glossary

## Introduction

This glossary gathers some of the most important concepts used throughout the tutorial.

## Electronic Identification

The process of identifying a person or entity in digital environments.

## Authentication

The process of verifying that a person, entity, or system is really the one it claims to be.

## Trust Service

An electronic service that supports trust in digital transactions, such as signing, sealing, timestamping, validation, preservation, or certified delivery.

## Trust Service Provider

A natural or legal person that provides one or more trust services.

## Qualified Trust Service Provider

A provider that has obtained qualified status under the eIDAS framework for one or more trust services.

## Electronic Signature

Data in electronic form attached to or logically associated with other electronic data and used by a signatory to sign.

## Advanced Electronic Signature

An electronic signature that meets reinforced requirements under eIDAS, especially regarding linkage to the signer and control over signing data.

## Qualified Electronic Signature

The reinforced form of electronic signature under eIDAS, based on a qualified certificate and created through the qualified framework.

## Electronic Seal

An electronic equivalent oriented to legal persons or entities rather than natural persons.

## Qualified Certificate

A certificate that meets the reinforced requirements set by eIDAS and is issued within the qualified framework.

## Trusted List

The official structured list used to identify qualified trust service providers and qualified trust services under a Member State's supervision.

## European Digital Identity Wallet

The wallet-based identity and credential model associated with the newer phase of the European framework.

## Summary

Understanding these terms first makes the rest of the tutorial much easier to follow.
