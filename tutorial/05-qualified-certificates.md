# 05. Qualified Certificates

## Introduction

Qualified certificates are one of the most important components in the eIDAS architecture. They are not the only relevant element in digital trust, but they play a foundational role in the qualified framework.

## What a Certificate Is

A certificate links verification data to a person, entity, or system within a trust framework.

In practice, certificates are commonly associated with:

- electronic signatures
- electronic seals
- website authentication

## What Makes a Certificate Qualified

A qualified certificate is not simply a better certificate in a general sense. It is a legal category under eIDAS.

This means that it:

- meets reinforced requirements
- is issued within the qualified framework
- is linked to qualified trust service provision

## Why It Matters

Qualified certificates matter because they support stronger legal and evidential positions within the eIDAS system.

Their relevance is especially visible in:

- qualified electronic signatures
- qualified electronic seals
- qualified website authentication certificates

## Practical Perspective

When a process depends on stronger legal assurance, it is not enough to ask whether a certificate exists. The key questions are:

- what kind of certificate it is
- who issued it
- whether it belongs to the qualified framework
- whether its status can be officially verified

## Relation to Providers

Qualified certificates cannot be understood in isolation from qualified trust service providers and the trusted-list system.

## Summary

Qualified certificates are a central building block of the reinforced eIDAS framework and should be assessed in connection with provider status, supervision, and official verification.
