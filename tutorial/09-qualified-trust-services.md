# 09. Qualified Trust Services

## Introduction

One of the central ideas in eIDAS is that not all trust services offer the same level of legal assurance. The regulation distinguishes between trust services in general and qualified trust services.

## What a Trust Service Is

A trust service is an electronic service related to trust-enhancing functions such as:

- signing
- sealing
- timestamping
- registered delivery
- website authentication
- validation and preservation

## What Qualified Means

The word `qualified` has a precise legal meaning in this context. It does not simply mean reliable or high quality in a commercial sense.

It means the service:

- belongs to the reinforced framework under eIDAS
- is provided under the qualified regime
- is connected to official supervision and verification

## Why Qualified Services Matter

Qualified services matter because they usually offer:

- stronger legal assurance
- stronger evidential positioning
- clearer cross-border trust conditions

## Relation to Providers

Qualified services must be understood together with qualified trust service providers and the trusted-list system.

## Practical Question

The core practical issue is not only whether a service works, but whether its legal and evidential profile is appropriate for the risk of the process.

## Summary

Qualified trust services represent the reinforced trust layer of the eIDAS system and are especially relevant in higher-risk or more regulated scenarios.
