# 07. Electronic Timestamps

## Introduction

Time is a decisive factor in digital evidence. In many cases, what matters is not only what happened, but also when it happened.

## What an Electronic Timestamp Is

An electronic timestamp links data to a particular moment in time.

Its function is not merely decorative. It helps support:

- temporal evidence
- integrity over time
- chronological traceability

## Why Timestamps Matter

Timestamps are useful when it is necessary to support:

- that a document existed at a given moment
- that data had a particular state at a certain time
- that a process step took place before or after another

## Qualified Timestamps

eIDAS also recognizes a qualified framework for timestamps. This matters in scenarios where stronger legal assurance or evidential robustness is needed.

## Common Misunderstanding

It is a mistake to think that a visible date on a document is equivalent to a trusted timestamp. The relevant issue is the trusted evidential mechanism behind the temporal claim.

## Summary

Electronic timestamps are key instruments for supporting time-based evidence in digital processes, especially where chronology and integrity are important.
