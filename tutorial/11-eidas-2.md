# 11. eIDAS 2.0 and the European Digital Identity Wallet

## Introduction

The reform commonly described as `eIDAS 2.0` marks a major new phase in the European digital identity and trust framework.

Its most visible development is the European Digital Identity Wallet, but the reform is broader than the wallet alone.

## Why This Reform Matters

The original eIDAS framework already provided a common structure for trust services and electronic identification. The reform expands the ambition of the system toward a more usable and interoperable identity model for citizens, residents, organizations, and service providers.

## The European Digital Identity Wallet

The wallet model is intended to support:

- electronic identification
- presentation of verified attributes
- controlled sharing of information
- broader use of trustworthy digital identity in both public and private services

## Not Just a Digital Folder

It is a mistake to think of the wallet as merely a place to store digital documents. The concept is wider and includes identity, credentials, attributes, and trust relationships between issuers, holders, and relying parties.

## Summary

eIDAS 2.0 expands the original framework and gives the European Digital Identity Wallet a central role in the next stage of digital trust in the EU.
