# 03. Trust Service Providers

## Introduction

Trust service providers are key actors in the eIDAS ecosystem. They issue certificates, support signing and sealing services, provide timestamps, offer registered delivery services, and participate in the wider infrastructure of digital trust.

## What a Trust Service Provider Is

In simple terms, a trust service provider is a natural or legal person that provides one or more trust services.

These may include:

- issuance of electronic certificates
- electronic signature services
- electronic seal services
- electronic timestamps
- registered electronic delivery
- validation and preservation services
- website authentication services

## Qualified and Non-Qualified Providers

The framework distinguishes between:

1. qualified trust service providers
2. non-qualified trust service providers

This is not a merely descriptive distinction. It affects supervision, legal assurance, and the evidential strength often associated with the service.

## Why Qualified Status Matters

Qualified status is a legal category. It is linked to reinforced requirements, supervision, and official listing in the trusted-list system where applicable.

That is why checking the official status of a provider matters more than relying on informal marketing claims.

## Trusted Lists

The trusted list system is one of the most practical tools in the eIDAS ecosystem because it helps verify:

- whether a provider is qualified
- which services are qualified
- the status of those services
- the supervising Member State

## Summary

Trust service providers make much of the eIDAS framework operational. Understanding their status is essential for both legal interpretation and practical implementation.
