# 04. Simple, Advanced, and Qualified Electronic Signatures

## Introduction

One of the most common sources of confusion in digital trust is the difference between basic electronic signing, advanced signatures, and qualified signatures.

## Electronic Signature as the Starting Point

The broad legal starting point is the `electronic signature`. In educational contexts, people often speak of a `simple electronic signature`, but the formal category in the regulation is the broader electronic signature.

## Advanced Electronic Signature

The advanced electronic signature is the reinforced form that meets specific requirements related to the signer, control, and the link between the signature and the signed data.

## Qualified Electronic Signature

The qualified electronic signature is the strongest formal category in the signature hierarchy. It is built within the qualified framework and benefits from a reinforced legal position under eIDAS.

## Why the Distinction Matters

The difference matters because not every digital signing process offers the same:

- legal certainty
- evidential robustness
- supervisory framework
- cross-border trust profile

## Practical View

The right choice depends on context, not just on labels. Lower-risk processes may tolerate simpler mechanisms, while higher-risk or more regulated scenarios may require stronger trust and evidential support.

## Summary

Electronic signatures are not all the same. The distinction between broader electronic signatures, advanced signatures, and qualified signatures is one of the key building blocks of the eIDAS system.
