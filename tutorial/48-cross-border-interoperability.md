# 48. Cross-Border Interoperability and Recognition

## Introduction

One of the most important goals of eIDAS is not only to regulate trust-related mechanisms, but also to make them work across Member States in a meaningful legal and technical way.

This is why two ideas are central:

- interoperability
- cross-border recognition

## What Interoperability Means Here

In this context, interoperability means that systems, providers, authorities, verifiers, and users can understand and use identification and trust mechanisms in a compatible way.

It is not only about whether a file exists or a certificate can be displayed. It is also about whether the surrounding information can be verified and understood in another jurisdiction.

## Why Cross-Border Recognition Matters

Cross-border recognition is essential for:

- international business relations
- mobility of citizens and professionals
- digital contracting between parties in different Member States
- access to public services from another Member State

## Trusted Lists and Supervision

Trusted lists and national supervision play a major role because they make it possible to verify the official status of qualified providers and services across the EU.

## Summary

Without interoperability and cross-border recognition, digital trust would remain mostly national. eIDAS aims to make it genuinely European.
