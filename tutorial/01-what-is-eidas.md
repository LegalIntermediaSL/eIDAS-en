# 01. What the eIDAS Regulation Is

## Introduction

The eIDAS framework is one of the central legal instruments of the European Union for digital trust. Its full title refers to electronic identification and trust services for electronic transactions in the internal market.

In practice, eIDAS is important because it provides a common legal framework for how electronic identities, signatures, seals, timestamps, delivery mechanisms, and related trust services can operate across the EU.

## What eIDAS Regulates

In broad terms, eIDAS covers two major areas:

- electronic identification
- trust services

Trust services include, among others:

- electronic signatures
- electronic seals
- electronic timestamps
- registered electronic delivery services
- website authentication certificates

## Why It Matters

Without a common framework, each Member State could rely on very different legal assumptions, technical approaches, and evidential practices.

eIDAS helps reduce fragmentation by creating common categories, shared rules, and a more interoperable environment for digital trust across Europe.

## A Practical Way to Read It

At a practical level, eIDAS is not only about technology. It is also about:

- legal certainty
- evidence
- supervision
- interoperability
- cross-border recognition

## Relation to eIDAS 2.0

The original regulation has now evolved through the reform commonly referred to as `eIDAS 2.0`, especially in connection with the European Digital Identity Wallet.

## Summary

eIDAS is the European legal framework that supports digital trust through common rules on electronic identification and trust services.
