# 08. Registered Electronic Delivery

## Introduction

Many digital disputes are not only about the content of a document, but also about whether something was sent, received, or made available.

## What Registered Electronic Delivery Is

Registered electronic delivery is a trust-service concept aimed at providing evidence related to electronic transmission.

It is particularly relevant for:

- proof of sending
- proof of receipt
- integrity of transmitted data

## Why It Matters

This kind of service becomes especially important where communication itself may later be challenged.

Typical examples include:

- formal notices
- contractual communications
- regulated communications
- evidential delivery processes

## Qualified Registered Delivery

Within eIDAS there is also a reinforced qualified framework for registered electronic delivery services.

## Practical Caution

Not every email system with tracking features amounts to a trust service in the eIDAS sense. Marketing labels should not be confused with legal categories.

## Summary

Registered electronic delivery helps support digital communications from an evidential perspective, especially when proof of transmission and receipt matters.
